Are you an introvert? Here's why you should be speaking up in class

October 5, 2016 | Shenelle Wilkins, Guidance Counselor

Being quiet in class isn't necessarily a bad thing. At least you get out of trouble, right? But there will be times when speaking up in class will actually be to your benefit.
You could be missing out a lot if you choose to keep quiet all the time and let your classmates answer all the questions for you.
How would you learn all the answers to the questions if all of the quiet students were put into one class?
Here's some advice that can help you out:





1. Don't be afraid to speak out

Remember, speaking out is always a good thing. It'll help others around you understand you and be better able to communicate with you as a result.

2. Everyone makes mistakes

Nobody's perfect. Don't go to class expecting to get all the answers right every time your name gets called.

3. School is a learning environment

If you get something wrong, that's okay. What's important is that you at least tried to speak up when others around might have been too afraid or shy.

4. The laughter of other students' laughter isn't the end of the world

You might get something wrong and may end up feeling embarrassed afterwards. Just remember that that's all part of the school experience. Sometimes you just have to laugh with those around you. A day without laughter is one that's wasted.

5. Don't feel the need to impress others

The minute you start to do something hoping that someone will notice or be impressed, you'll find yourself in ruin. If you're not so worried about impressing others, you won't stress so much over it and have better concentration.

The most important factor in all this is confidence. Don't expect to become extremely confident in yourself over night. At the same time, don't allow yourself to become too overconfident, either. Remember, trying your best and learning from your mistakes is what school is all about.
