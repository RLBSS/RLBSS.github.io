On Your Mark, Get Set, Go!

January 23, 2017 | Sydney Williams, Vice Principal

Our Sports Term is finally here! At this time, all of our best athletes are expected to be preparing themselves to support their respective houses.
For those who are having a hard time, here are a few tips that you can try to follow some simple steps and guidelines that every athlete must follow.
Remember, you can always feel free to visit Mr. Collin if you feel like you need proper training and advice.





1. Organize your time wisely!

Remember, you're still a student. As much as it is important for you to devote time to training and making other preparations, you should still keep in mind that you still have to commit to your academic success. Take note of all important dates for homework and tests as well the time you have to practice and prepare yourself.

2. Eat healthy and sleep well

This is the most important time for you to get all the nutrients and sleep as you can get. It's important that you maintain good health practices so that you can be in peak condition for any activity. Eating right is not limited to giving you energy. It also supplies your body with nutrients that can help your bones, muscles and other tissues. Getting enough sleep will also help your body to rest up, helping you to perform better the next time. It is of utmost importance that all athletes maintain a balanced lifestyle with proper nutrition and adequate resting periods.

3. Always be prepared for failure

All great athletes fail at some point in their lives. Always be prepared for any possible outcome. Being properly prepared can help you spring back on your feet quickly no matter what the outcome. More often than not, the possibility of losing is always ignored by both amateur and professional athletes. Not everyone will be a winner at the end of the sporting events. Therefore, it is important that all athletes know how to cope with failure and disappointment. Losing is always a posibility and it should not be seen as the end or a mental block. Students should view losing as a motivation for devoting more time and effort into preparing themselves for the next time. Doing so will not only help you in sports but throughout your entire life.

4. Overcoming your inhibitions

An important skill for every athlete to learn is how to stay calm and cool under pressure. In doing so, you will be able to focus on the day of the competition and perform better as a result. Allowing yourself to get too nervous or over-excited just before an important event can be a distraction and can also put you at a disadvantage. Losing your cool can cause you to feel pressured and prevent your body from being naturally relaxed. When this happens, your entire body and performance is affected. If not handled carefully, you may lose the competition not because you were not adequately prepared physically, but because you were not properly prepared mentally. In the end, mental preparation should not be neglected and is just as important as physical preparation.

5. Stay positive!

No matter what the outcome is, what's important is that you gave it your all at the end of the day. Accomplishing what you set yourself out to do is one of the greatest feelings you can have. Don't spend time dwelling on your mistakes to the point when it's the only thing on your mind. Remember, there will always be room for improvement for the next time. Until then, it is up to you as an athlete to understand that, while perfectionism is a great trait to have, it can also be a very destructive force that can take control of your life completely if you are not careful. Never give up!







We hope that all students participating in sports and other events do their best!
May the best man win!
